Discovering the Planets: A Journey Through Our Solar System.

~ 🪐 Planets: Our Neighbors in Space.

Planets are large celestial bodies that orbit stars. In our solar system, all planets revolve around one star — the Sun. Planets do not produce their own light; instead, they reflect sunlight. Each planet is unique in size, composition, and the number of moons it has.

~ 🌞 The Solar System: 

Our solar system has eight main planets, divided into two groups:

🪨 Terrestrial (Rocky) Planets:
1-Mercury – The smallest planet and closest to the Sun.

2-Venus – Extremely hot with thick clouds; known as Earth's "sister planet."

3-Earth – Our home and the only planet known to support life.

4-Mars – The red planet, famous for its dusty surface and potential for life.

~ 🌌 Gas Giants (Outer Planets): 


5-Jupiter – The largest planet, known for its Great Red Spot (a giant storm).

6-Saturn – Famous for its stunning rings.

7-Uranus – A tilted, icy planet with a pale blue-green color.

8-Neptune – The farthest planet from the Sun, with powerful winds and storms.

~ 🛰️ Are There Other Planets?

Yes! There are dwarf planets like Pluto, which are smaller and don’t meet all the criteria to be classified as full planets. Scientists have also discovered thousands of exoplanets—planets orbiting stars outside our solar system.
